Mohamed M. Kamra

I graduated from the aerospace department at the Faculty of Engineering, Cairo University where I got  my bachelor degree of engineering and master of science in 2010 and 2015 respectively. I was awarded a scholarship from the Japanese government (MEXT) to study violent fluid-structure interaction and I got my PhD. degree in computational fluid dynamics from Kyushu University in 2018.
In the same year, I received a fellowship to continue my research and study the effect of violent sloshing in LNG tanks with complex internal structures.
Later, I managed to secure an Assistant Professor position at the Research Institute for Applied Mechanics (RIAM) in Kyushu University where I have overseen several post-graduate  students.

Research Interests

CFD Simulation of two tandem tidal turbines. The effect of the water free surface is demonstrated in the presence of waves. The simulation is conducted using a CFD solver  developed in the open-source framework of OpenFOAM.

Simulation of the Rayleigh–Taylor instability problem.  An instability of an interface between two fluids of different densities which occurs when the lighter fluid is pushing the heavier fluid. The problem is solved using a solver developed in the pyFR high order framework.

Multi-phase simulation of the classical dambreak benchmark problem. In this problem, an obstacle is placed in the way of collapsed water column. The effect of the obstacle cross-section on the impact intensity is studied. The simulation is conducted using an in-house CFD solver  and compared to experimental data.

Dambreak benchmark case against a cuboid obstacle. This is a classical validation case for multi-phase solvers. This simulation was conducted using an in-house developed CFD solver  and computed on Kyushu University ITO super-computer.

CFD case study of cryogenic hazardous gas leaking from a container in closed space. The study demonstrates the flow pattern of the leaked and the effective of of proper ventilation. 

Numerical simulation of hazardous gas leaking from the vent of large ship at sea. The effect of wind speed and direction on the safety of the ship is examined here.
